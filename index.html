How a Commission Comes Together

From reference to graphite.

01

Consultation

Share a clear reference photo along with the size and format you have in mind. Every piece starts with a good source image.

02

Under-sketch

Proportions and structure are blocked in first, checked carefully against the reference before any shading begins.

03

Rendering

Layer by layer, graphite builds tone, texture, and likeness — the slowest and most deliberate stage.

04

Delivery

The finished piece is signed, photographed, and handed off — framed or unframed, your choice.

The Artist

Behind the pencil.

Khaakkhana began on paper and graphite, long before it became a name. Behind the pencil is Bilal — an energy systems engineering student by day, and a portrait artist by instinct.

Every commission is built up in layers: light, shadow, and the small imperfections that make a face recognizable rather than just resembled. No filters, no shortcuts — just graphite, patience, and an eye trained to notice what a photograph flattens.

Good to Know

Frequently asked.

Most pieces take 7–10 days from confirmed reference to finished sketch, depending on size and how full the queue is. Rush timelines can sometimes be arranged — ask when you reach out.

Clear, well-lit, and in focus — natural light works best. Straight-on or three-quarter angles render most reliably. Avoid heavy filters or very low-resolution images.

Local clients can pay via bank transfer, EasyPaisa, or JazzCash (PKR equivalent). International clients can pay via bank transfer or other agreed methods. A deposit secures your place in the queue, with the remainder due once the piece is finished and approved.

Small adjustments during the under-sketch stage are no problem. Once rendering begins, major changes to pose or composition may affect the timeline.
